step one

Answer 7 prompts on a voice memo

Use the full worksheet for this part. It has a text box and a "copy for Claude" button for each prompt. This tab is your quick-reference while you talk it through live.
01

What is it, exactly?

Name, price, format, and what they get the moment they buy.

"50 A Day is a $27 self-paced mini course that walks through..."

02

Where is she stuck right now?

Her frustration in her own words. What's she tried that hasn't worked?

"She's posting daily and getting nothing back for it..."

03

What changes once she's done this?

The specific, tangible result. Not a vague feeling.

"She'll have 50 new subscribers a day without touching social..."

04

Walk me through what's included

Every module, template, or bonus, and what it helps her do.

"Module 1 covers list building without ads, Module 2 is..."

05

Who should buy this, and who shouldn't?

Stage of business, what they already know, who should skip it.

"This is for coaches with an existing list who want to grow it..."

06

What's she thinking right before she closes the tab?

Say the doubts out loud, then answer them like a DM reply.

"She'll worry it's too advanced, but really..."

07

Why did you make this, and why now?

The real story, any proof it works, and any urgency or deadline.

"I built this after testing it with cold Meta traffic myself..."

Don't overthink it. Messy and natural beats scripted every time. Claude is doing the polishing, not you.

One message. Template, transcript, prompt.

This is the only message where you attach everything at once: the template file and your voice transcript, plus the prompt below pasted into the message box. Sending it all together keeps this to a single heavy message instead of several.

I'm attaching a sales page template and a transcript where I talk through my offer. Use the template as the base and replace every placeholder with real copy pulled from my transcript. step three

One thorough pass beats five small ones

Open your first draft, start a voice memo, and scroll through the whole page talking through every change you want. If a section's fine, say so and move on. Cover the entire page in one recording rather than sending Claude five separate small requests. It keeps the back-and-forth short and the page reads better because everything gets updated with the full picture in mind.

Start a new chat for this. Upload the draft file fresh instead of continuing the same conversation. It keeps things quick.

walk through each of these on your recording

1

Headline & hero

Read it out loud. Does it sound like you? Is the price right?

"Change the headline to... the price should say $27, not $47."

2

Pain points

Do they match what your buyer actually feels, in her words?

"Swap the second one for: she's tried three freebies and nothing stuck."

3

What's included

Are the names and descriptions actually accurate?

"Feature 2 should be called The Vault, not The Template Library."

4

Your story

Does it sound like you? Are the numbers right?

"Change 6,000 subscribers to closer to 6,200. I moved to Flodesk in June, not May."

5

FAQ

Are these the real objections you actually hear? Anything missing?

"Add one: does this work if I don't have a list yet?"

6

Everything else

Buttons, closing section, anything that still reads generic.

"Last CTA should say: Grab it before the price goes up."
